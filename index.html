The Third Man

"A film to behold"

What is The Third Man about?

The Third Man is a movie based on a book of the same name which follows the story of an American writer, Holly Martins who is invited across the Atlantic ocean to a divided post-World War 2 Vienna by his friend, Harry Lime. Holly upon arrival in the divided Vienna, comes to find Harry is not at his apartment. According to the keeper of the complex, Lime had suffered a terrible car accident at the hands of his own driver.

Having flown across the ocean to a promised job, the only business Martins had in Vienna was to attend his dearest friends funeral. This is where he would meet Major Calloway and his assistant, on top of seeing Lime's girlfriend and friends. After meeting and conversing with one of Lime's friends, he finds discrepencies between his story and that of the keepers. Becoming suspicious of Harry's death, Holly takes it upon himself to find out what really happened to Harry Lime.

Though I would like to continue to tell the tale of The Third Man, it cannot be appreciated fully without watching the classic film in its entirety.

Thus, I leave the rest of the story untold to be unraveled by your own eyes.

The Third Man Trivia

  • Orson Welles only worked on this film for one week.
  • This movie is the favorite film of Breaking Bad creator Vince Gilligan.
  • The ending of the movie differs from the book, however the author of the book acknowledges the ending of the movie to be better than that of the book.
  • Despite the fact that this was the most popular film in the UK during its release year, Austria did not care for it.
  • Although some of the most famous shots of the movie were in the Vienna sewers, Welles refused to enter the sewers because of the smell.
  • The film has a permanent slot in one of Vienna's oldest theaters, the "Burgkino" where it plays three times a week.
  • Orson Welles voices a radio series named "The lives of Harry Lime", after his character in the film.
  • David O. Selznick though a producer, was hated by the rest of the crew and brought ideas deemed unfavorable.
  • The film sits at #1 on the British Film Institutes list of the 100 best British films ever.
  • The keeper of the apartment where Harry lived did not speak a word of english, and learned his lines phoenetically.
  • Acllaimed film critic Roger Ebert has listed this as his favorite film.
  • All the rubble and demolished buildings in Vienna throughout the film are not artifical, only a result of the WWII bombings on the city.
